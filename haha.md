**Call Me Libertad: Poems Between Borders**  
Poetry and Art Anthology  

This multilingual book combines the voices of people in immigration detention with their family members and allies to give insight into immigration detention, positioning them as experts in their own experiences. It grew out of a writing workshop taught by Alicia Partnoy for the organization CIVIC (Community Initiatives for Visiting Immigrants in Confinement). The book is an effort to liberate our political imagination so that we may build together a country without immigration detention.

**Transcending Borders: The Central Valley Writers' Premio Literario**  
Central Valley, California  
Writing Award & Anthology Publication  
[https://arteamericas.org/mission/](https://arteamericas.org/mission/)

Arte Américas and the Central Valley Chicana and Chicano Writers Collective initiated this Premio Literario to empower youth (ages 18-24) to share their immigrant experiences through writing. The program aims to counter false narratives and overcome fear and anxiety in the current political climate by highlighting stories of joy, hope, fear, laughter, and persistence. The collective writes because they could not find books with characters who looked like them as children, and they now make their stories available so readers of all ages can discover stories with characters they can relate to.

Click on it: “We are facing difficult times, as a new administration challenges the very existence of a diverse community of immigrants. While redadas, deportation roundups, have brought terror and insecurity to our immigrant communities, we are reminded of the importance of telling our own stories. Documenting our stories is a vital role for us as a community, especially when our very existence is being questioned. ... We believe humans are story-telling beings, and from childhood we have hungered for stories just as we hungered for food. These are some of the stories that have been untold. We see these young writers, like ourselves, helping to address the shortage of stories about our immigrant experiences, an essential part of the American story. When we share the stories of our lives we become writers. If we are silent our stories won't be told, and they deserve to be told and shared with others.”

**Boyle Heights Beat / LAist**  
East Los Angeles, California  
Community-Funded Nonprofit News  
[https://laist.com](https://laist.com)

Boyle Heights Beat is a community-funded, youth-focused nonprofit news outlet serving the East Los Angeles neighborhood. As a publication of the USC Annenberg School of Journalism, it trains local youth to report on stories that matter to their community, stories often overlooked by mainstream media. LAist amplifies this work as part of its commitment to local journalism.

Ex: East LA Healing Circle: Turning ICE Traumatic Experiences into Healing  
This story centers how a community responded: women and children gathering for a healing circle, organizing patrols, tracking ICE activity, and offering ancestral medicine and spiritual support. The coverage does not linger on spectacle. Instead, it documents resilience, mutual aid, and the work of healing.

**California Community Foundation (CCF) / LA Plaza Cultura y Artes / Japanese American National Museum**  
Los Angeles, California  
Public Art Campaign & Community Solidarity Project  
https://www.aminextla.org

The "Am I Next?" campaign projects 20 by 30-foot portraits of Angelenos onto buildings across downtown LA to confront ICE raids and attacks on civil liberties. The campaign draws direct parallels between current immigration enforcement and the WWII incarceration of Japanese Americans, harnessing art as resistance and coalition-building across communities.

Example of Justice-Oriented Coverage: Instead of turning immigrant suffering into spectacle, this campaign centers solidarity, historical memory, and collective action.
