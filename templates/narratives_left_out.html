Conclusion

What reporting resilience does, or at least what we hope it does, is introduce our audience to resources and organizations they may not have previously been aware of, and provide the impacted communities with a sense of empowerment and a renewed trust in their own power and voices. Further, we aim to make the general public aware that ICE’s control and impact is not as far-reaching as the agency itself, as well as our current administration, would like to make it seem. We illustrate this by highlighting various instances of resistance, such as: legal support, know your rights workshops, food/supply drives, among many others. Our project mainly focuses on organizations based in California (except for Immigrant Defense Project, which is based in New York) largely due to us only having been able to speak with community organizers within this state. This is, admittedly, one of our project’s limitations. However, we believe that our project’s core message still remains strong. By including the perspectives and experiences of the communities that are directly impacted, as well as carefully and intentionally selecting certain pieces of data and information, we have carefully curated these narratives to ensure our reporting is both ethical, effective, and factually rigorous.
